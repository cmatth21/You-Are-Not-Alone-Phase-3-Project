Resources and Research

Below are a few resources to get additional help. Each numbered bullet links to their corresponding websites.

  1. Crisis Prevention

    If you or anyone you know have had suicidal thoughts or intentions of harm, please call the the National Suicide Prevention Line at 1-800-273-TALK(8255). For people who are deaf or hard of hearing, please dial 711 before the hotline number or visit this link to chat with a counselor.

  2. Substance Abuse and Mental Health

    The Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration provides links and additional resources to get help with substance use disorders.

  3. Coping with Being Alone

    Sometimes we forget how to be alone with ourselves. The link above has plenty of tips about coping with loneliness.

  4. Find a Therapist

    Psychology Today is an online platform that allows users to search for professional help in the form of a therapist, psychiatrist, therapy groups, etc. There are many different filters to help with finding someone or a group that fits you best.

  5. Psychedelic Research

    Did you know that there is a booming interest in psychedelics research and the beneficial effects of psychedelics on mental health conditions? Johns Hopkins University has been conducting studies on treating mental health conditions with psilocybin.

  6. EMDR Therapy

    Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR) therapy is a that has been helping countless individuals treat post-traumatic stress disorder, depression, trauma, anxiety, etc.
