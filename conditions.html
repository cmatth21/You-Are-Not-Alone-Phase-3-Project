Mental Health Conditions

Mental health issues are very common. According to mentalhealth.gov, one in five Americans have experienced a mental health condition. More awareness about mental health conditions can help reduce common myths and stigmas associated with them. For more information about these conditions, please seek out a medical profesional.

Mood Disorders

Mood disorders typically affect a person's emotional state. They may include feeling a large range of emotions from extreme happiness to extreme sadness. Mood disorders, also known as affective disorders include: depression, Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD), or bipolar disorder. For more information, visit MentalHealth.gov.

Anxiety Disorders

Although anxiety is generally experienced by everyone at some point or another temporarily, some people experience anxiety on a more permanent basis and often times it does not fade away. Temporary anxiety may be caused by stress from work or exams in school but people who have anxiety disorders may not have a reason for it. Anxiety disorders may include, general anxiety disorder (GAD), phobias, or panic disorders. For more information, visit the National Institute of Mental Health website.

Psychosis

According to the National Alliance of Mental Illness, psychosis makes it difficult to discern what is real to what is not due to thought or perception disruptions. It is very important to get help immediately if someone exhibits symptoms showing a loss of touch with reality. For more information, please visit NAMI.org.

Eating Disorders

Eating disorders are illness that cause disturbances in eating habits that negatively affect ones' physical health. They may include: anorexia nervosa, bulemia, or binge-eating. People with eating disorders may be extremely concerned about body image, weight, and food. For more information, please visit psychiatry.org.
